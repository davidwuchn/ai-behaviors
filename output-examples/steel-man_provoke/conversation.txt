❯ Implement a snake game clone in python3 with pygame. Use venv to create a 
   virtual environment. Rules of the game                                   
  - board is 8x8                                                            
  - snake starts size 3 in the middle, moving right                         
  - there's always an apple on the board; eating it grows the snake by 1    
  - occasionally a star spawns; eating it grows the snake by 3; if uneaten  
  the star disappears after 20 turns; there's only at most 1 star present   
  at every point                                                            
  - the speed of the game increases after every 10 points                   
  - the user can see the current points and speed                           
  - the snake moves with wasd or arrow keys                                 
  - the game starts pressing enter; on game end enter starts a new game     
  - there's no leaderboard or persistence                                   
  - key presses stack - e.g. if the user presses "wd" rapidly (faster than  
  game speed) the snake will turn up on first turn, right on second         
  - self collision is not allowed - turning left when moving right is       
  impossible; this applies to stacked moves as well                         
  - the game starts through a `snake` executable                            
                                                                            
  #steel-man #provoke                                                       

● Before I build this, let me shake the tree on the design.

  Steel-manning the 8x8 board: It's actually a sharp design choice. Small board
  means every cell matters immediately. There's no boring "roaming around an
  empty field" phase. Tension arrives in the first 30 seconds. The star (+3)
  becomes a genuinely agonizing risk/reward decision because 3 cells is ~5% of
  the entire board. Games are short enough for "just one more." The input queue
  mechanic is legitimately good UX — it prevents the infuriating "I pressed the
  key but the game ate it" problem that plagues most snake implementations. And
  validating queued moves against self-collision is the kind of detail that
  separates a polished game from a frustrating one.

  The flaw even in that strongest version: 61 free cells after the starting
  snake means ~15 apples and a couple of stars puts you at half-capacity, where
  games stop being about skill and start being about whether the apple spawns
  somewhere reachable. You've built a game that trains players to get good, then
   kills them with RNG. A 12x12 board gives the same escalating tension but
  enough room for skillful routing to matter at high scores. But — you spec'd
  8x8, and the fast-burn arcade argument holds, so 8x8 it is.
